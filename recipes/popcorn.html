Microwave Popcorn

Description

Okay, so hear me out. I know this is not a soup recipe as I originally intended. It is indeed a short list of ingredients, but that's not why I choose it either. I chose this recipe for my dad, who loved popcorn.

This recipe is also taken from allrecipes.com. It doesn't have any functional value to me as I have found and made use of my dad's air popper, which we used when I was a kid. Maybe it's becuases the air popper takes up quite a bit of counter space or just doesn't keep up in speed and convenience, but, in his old age, my father preferred microwae popcorn. I don't have any packs of it in the kitchen I inherited from him, but I do have the "brown paper lunch sack" as listed in the instructions. My father's purpose in keeping such a sack is mysterious, as he preferred to keep his lunch in a cooler.

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup unpopped popcorn
  • 1 teaspoon vegetable oil
  • 1/2 teaspoon salt, or to taste

Directions

  1. Gather all ingredients.
  2. Mix together unpopped popcorn and oil in a cup or small bowl.
  3. Pour the coated corn into a brown paper lunch sack, and sprinkle in the salt. Fold the top of the bag over twice to seal in the ingredients.
  4. Cook in the microwave at full power for 2 1/2 to 3 minutes, or until you hear pauses of about 2 seconds between pops. Carefully open the bag to avoid steam, and pour into a serving bowl.
  5. Enjoy!
